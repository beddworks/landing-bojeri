# Accounting
> Your books, balanced — customers, vendors, banking, and reports in one place.

## The Problem
When your accounting lives in a separate tool from your invoicing, purchasing, and payroll, you spend more time reconciling data than actually understanding your finances. Manual journal entries, disconnected bank records, and no real-time reporting mean you're always working with yesterday's numbers — and month-end closes take days instead of hours.

## How It Works
Bojeri's Accounting module brings full double-entry bookkeeping directly into your ERP. Start with a **Chart of Accounts** organized by type — Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenue, and Expenses. Record **Revenue** and **Expenses** with categories, dates, amounts, and reference numbers. Manage **Customer Payments** and **Vendor Payments** with clear tracking of what's been received and what's been disbursed. The **Banking** section lets you manage **Bank Accounts**, record **Bank Transactions**, and process **Bank Transfers** between accounts. Handle disputes and adjustments with **Credit Notes** (for customers) and **Debit Notes** (for vendors) — both linked to their source transactions. Access **Reports** for profit and loss, balance sheets, and transaction summaries. The **System Setup** configures your accounting categories, payment methods, and chart structure.

## Key Benefits
- **Maintain a full Chart of Accounts** organized by account type for proper double-entry bookkeeping
- **Track revenue and expenses** with categorization, dates, amounts, and reference numbers
- **Manage banking** with multiple bank accounts, transaction recording, and inter-account transfers
- **Process customer payments** and track what's been received against outstanding invoices
- **Handle vendor payments** and monitor disbursements against purchase invoices
- **Issue credit notes** for customer adjustments and **debit notes** for vendor returns
- **Generate financial reports** — profit and loss, balances, and transaction summaries
- **Keep everything connected** — accounting data links to invoices, purchases, and payroll

## Who This Is For
**Finance Directors** — Maintain accurate books with double-entry accounting and real-time reports.
**Bookkeepers** — Record transactions, reconcile bank accounts, and manage payments daily.
**Business Owners** — See revenue, expenses, and cash flow without hiring a full accounting team.

## Real-World Example
A catering company uses Bojeri Accounting to manage their finances across 50+ events per month. They've set up their Chart of Accounts with asset accounts for their three bank accounts, liability accounts for taxes payable, and revenue accounts split by "Corporate Events" and "Private Events." When a client pays Invoice #1042, the bookkeeper records a Customer Payment — the revenue account updates automatically. When they pay their food vendor, they create a Vendor Payment linked to the purchase invoice. At month end, the Finance Director pulls the Profit and Loss report and sees exactly which event category drives the most revenue — without exporting a single spreadsheet.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: Does accounting data sync with invoicing and purchasing?**
A: Yes. The Accounting module is integrated with Sales Invoices, Purchase Invoices, and Returns. Payments, credit notes, and debit notes are all linked to their source transactions for seamless reconciliation.

**Q: Can I manage multiple bank accounts?**
A: Absolutely. Create as many bank accounts as you need, record transactions against each one, and transfer between them — all within the Banking section.
