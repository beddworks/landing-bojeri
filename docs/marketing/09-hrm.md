# HRM
> Manage your people — attendance, payroll, leaves, and growth — all in one place.

## The Problem
HR management through spreadsheets means leave requests get lost in email, attendance is tracked on paper, payroll calculations are error-prone, and employee milestones go unrecognized. When your people data is scattered, you can't make informed decisions about shifts, salaries, or workforce planning — and your employees feel the friction.

## How It Works
Bojeri's HRM module covers the complete employee lifecycle. Start by adding **Employees** with personal details, employment information, department, designation, and pay rate. Set up **Shifts** with start times, end times, break periods, and night shift support. Employees track attendance through **Clock In / Clock Out** — the system automatically calculates total working hours, break hours, overtime, and attendance status (Present, Half Day, Absent) based on their shift schedule. **IP restriction** ensures clock-ins only happen from authorized locations. Manage **Leave Types**, handle **Leave Applications** with approval workflows, and track **Leave Balances** and **Holidays**. Configure salaries through **Set Salary** with allowances and deductions, then run **Payroll** and generate **Payslips**. Beyond payroll, track **Awards**, **Promotions**, **Resignations**, **Terminations**, **Warnings**, and **Complaints** for complete employee lifecycle management. Send **Announcements**, manage **Events**, track **Documents** and **Acknowledgments**, and handle internal **Transfers** between departments. The **System Setup** configures departments, designations, leave types, and other HR parameters.

## Key Benefits

## Real-World Example
A logistics company with 120 drivers and warehouse staff uses Bojeri HRM. Shifts are configured: "Morning" (6 AM–2 PM with 30-min break) and "Night" (10 PM–6 AM, flagged as night shift). Drivers clock in from the warehouse IP address — if someone tries from home, the system blocks it. When driver Ravi works 10 hours on his 8-hour Morning shift, the system auto-calculates 2 hours of overtime at his $18/hour rate — $36 in overtime pay. At month end, payroll runs with all attendance data, overtime amounts, and leave deductions already calculated. The HR manager just reviews and approves. When warehouse lead Sarah earns Employee of the Month, she gets an Award entry. When she's promoted to Logistics Coordinator, the promotion record tracks her old and new role, effective date, and salary change.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: How does the overtime calculation work?**
A: The system compares actual working hours (clock in to clock out, minus breaks) against the standard shift hours. Any excess is overtime. If the employee has a per-hour rate configured, the overtime amount is calculated automatically.

**Q: Can employees manage their own attendance and leaves?**
A: Yes. Employees with the appropriate permissions can clock in/out through their dashboard and submit leave applications. Approvals are handled by managers with the corresponding permission.
