# Proposals
> Turn quotes into commitments — then into invoices — in clicks, not days.

## The Problem
Creating a professional proposal usually means digging through old documents, manually calculating line items, and emailing PDFs back and forth hoping the client responds. When they finally say yes, you start the whole process again to create an invoice. It's slow, error-prone, and every hour spent on admin is an hour not spent closing the next deal.

## How It Works
Create a proposal by selecting a customer, choosing products from your warehouse inventory, and adding line items with quantities, prices, discounts, and taxes. The system auto-generates a unique proposal number and calculates subtotals, tax amounts, discount amounts, and the final total. Every proposal follows a clear status lifecycle: **Draft → Sent → Accepted → Rejected**. When you're ready, mark the proposal as Sent — an event fires to notify the customer. Once accepted, convert the proposal directly into a Sales Invoice with a single click — all line items, taxes, customer details, and notes carry over automatically. Print-ready views let you share polished proposals with your branding.

## Key Benefits
- **Auto-calculate totals** including per-line discounts, multi-rate taxes, and running subtotals
- **Track proposal status** through a clear Draft → Sent → Accepted → Rejected lifecycle
- **Convert to invoice instantly** — one click creates a Sales Invoice with all line items and taxes preserved
- **Pull products from warehouses** with live stock quantities so you never quote items you can't deliver
- **Print proposals** with a clean, professional layout ready for clients
- **Attach per-item taxes** with named tax rates for full tax compliance
- **Filter and search** proposals by status, customer, date, and proposal number
- **Control access** — separate permissions for creating, editing, viewing, sending, accepting, and rejecting

## Who This Is For
**Sales Teams** — Create and send polished proposals in minutes, right from the product catalog.
**Business Owners** — Track which proposals are pending, accepted, or rejected to forecast revenue.
**Clients** — Review proposals shared with them and accept or reject directly through the platform.

## Real-World Example
Fatima runs a furniture supply company. A hotel chain requests a quote for 200 office chairs and 50 conference tables. She opens Proposals, selects the hotel as the customer, picks "Downtown Warehouse," and the system shows her available stock — 320 chairs and 75 tables. She adds both items, applies a 5% volume discount, and the system calculates the total with 11% VAT automatically. She clicks "Send" and the hotel receives notification. Two days later, the hotel accepts. Fatima clicks "Convert to Invoice" and a complete Sales Invoice appears with every line item, discount, tax, and note already filled in — ready to post.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: What happens to the proposal after it's converted to an invoice?**
A: The proposal is marked as "converted" and linked to the new invoice. The original proposal is preserved for your records, and the invoice gets its own independent lifecycle.

**Q: Can clients accept or reject proposals themselves?**
A: Yes. Clients with the client role can view proposals shared with them and accept or reject them directly through the platform.
