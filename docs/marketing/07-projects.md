# Projects
> Plan, track, and deliver — every task, every milestone, every team member.

## The Problem
Projects collapse when tasks live in emails, bug reports sit in chat messages, and milestones are scribbled on whiteboards. No one knows who's doing what, what's blocked, and whether the project will land on time. You need a workspace where tasks, bugs, files, and progress are visible to everyone who matters — without the overhead of a standalone project management tool.

## How It Works
Create a project with a name, description, start date, end date, budget, estimated hours, and status (Not Started, In Progress, On Hold, Completed, Cancelled). Invite **team members** and **clients** to provide visibility. Break work down into **Tasks** with titles, descriptions, priorities (High, Medium, Low), due dates, assigned team members, milestones, and custom stage workflows. Tasks support **subtasks** with individual due dates and completion tracking, plus **comments** for threaded discussions. Move tasks between stages with drag-and-drop on the **Kanban board** or view them in a **Calendar view**. Track defects with the dedicated **Bug tracker** — bugs have priorities, stages, assignments, and their own comment threads. Upload **project files** for centralized document management. Every action is tracked in the **Activity Log** for complete project history. Generate **Project Reports** to analyze progress across your portfolio.

## Key Benefits
- **Manage the full project lifecycle** from Not Started through In Progress, On Hold, to Completed
- **Break work into tasks** with priorities, due dates, milestones, and multi-member assignments
- **Track subtasks** within each task for granular progress visibility
- **Discuss in context** with threaded comments on tasks and bugs
- **Visualize on Kanban boards** — drag and drop tasks and bugs between custom stages
- **Plan with Calendar view** for timeline-based task management
- **Track bugs separately** with dedicated stages, priorities, and comment threads
- **Upload project files** to keep documents, designs, and deliverables in one place
- **Invite clients** to give them visibility into project progress without exposing internal details
- **Generate reports** across your project portfolio for executive-level insights
- **Duplicate projects** to reuse structures for recurring project types

## Who This Is For
**Project Managers** — Plan, assign, and track every task from kickoff to delivery in one workspace.
**Development Teams** — Use the bug tracker and Kanban boards to manage sprints and defect resolution.
**Clients** — View project progress, milestones, and deliverables through invited access.

## Real-World Example
A digital agency lands a website redesign project. The project manager creates the project in Bojeri with a $15,000 budget, 200 estimated hours, and a 6-week timeline. She sets up milestones: "Discovery," "Design," "Development," and "Launch." Under each milestone, she creates tasks — "Stakeholder interviews" under Discovery, "Homepage wireframe" under Design — each with assigned team members and due dates. The designer uploads mockups as project files. When the QA tester finds a broken form, he files a Bug with "High" priority that lands on the Kanban board. The whole team watches tasks flow from "To Do" to "In Progress" to "Done." The client, invited to the project, logs in to see milestone progress without needing a status update email.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: Can clients see everything in the project?**
A: Clients are invited separately from team members and have their own permission set. You control exactly what they can see — typically milestones and progress, but not internal bugs or task comments unless you grant access.

**Q: Can I customize the task and bug stages?**
A: Yes. Both task stages and bug stages are fully customizable. You can add, rename, reorder, and remove stages to match your team's workflow.
